# ZLTAC — Zone Laser Tag Australasian Championship
## Complete Event History Reference (1999–2026)

*Source: Doug Burbidge's tournament archive (dougburbidge.com/OzNationals)*

The Australasian championship has been held annually since 1999 as the Zone 3 / Darkzone laser tag national tournament. It was officially renamed **ZLTAC** in 2014. There was no event in 2021 due to the COVID-19 pandemic; the Brisbane Rumble was held as an unofficial alternative.

## Cool Facts and Patterns

**Three-peat dynasties (Team event):**
- WA Knights — 2011, 2012 (back-to-back; 3rd in 2010, 2nd in 2013)
- Brisbane Maroons — 2015, 2016, 2017
- Wolfpack — 2018, 2019, 2020

**Back-to-back team champions:**
- Hobart A — 2003, 2004
- Vikings — 2023, 2024

**Legendary repeat winners across divisions:**
- **Bootza** — Solos champion three years running (2015, 2016, 2017); Masters champion in 2012, 2013, 2015, 2019; multiple Doubles and Triples wins. One of the most decorated players in ZLTAC history.
- **CV (CaptainVegetable)** — Won an extraordinary four divisions at the 2023 event (Solos, Doubles, Triples, Masters); LotR winner 2014 & 2015; Doubles winner with Runt in 2022, 2023, 2024; Masters champion 2020, 2022, 2023, 2024.
- **Chuch** — Womens champion 2017, 2020, 2022, 2023.
- **Sinclair / Gwai Lo / Belfast** — Triples champions in 2005, 2009, and 2010 — the longest-running successful Triples lineup in ZLTAC history.
- **Chunky & Slimey** — Doubles champions three years running (2009, 2010, 2011).

**Format evolution:**
- 1999: Team only
- 2000: Doubles and Solos added
- 2005: Triples, Masters, and Womens added (six divisions)
- 2012: Lord of the Rings (LotR) added
- 2016: Juniors added (eight divisions — current format)

**Tournament name:**
- 1999–2013: Zone 3 / Darkzone Australian Nationals
- 2014–present: ZLTAC (Zone Laser Tag Australasian Championship)

**Pandemic gap:** 2021 is the only year with no official event since the championship began. The Brisbane Rumble was held as an unofficial alternative.

**Notable player names that appear repeatedly across decades:** Bootza, CV, Sinclair, Gwai Lo, Belfast, Slimey, Chunky, Damo, Hybrid, Mopar, GMI, Raven, Beefy, Wax, Husky, Runt, Pugsalad, Spitfire — many have spans of 10+ years competing.

**Tournament size growth:** From 4 teams in 1999 to ~24 teams in 2026 — a 6× increase over 27 years. Recent years (2022 onwards) have consistently seen 20+ teams.
